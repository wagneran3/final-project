Club Volleyball

At NVVA we define club volleyball as competitive teams for intermediate to advanced skill level athletes that are ready to invest more time into volleyball. Club volleyball teams have a busier practice and tournament schedule and longer season than the usual league.

If you are not familiar with club volleyball age groups and levels, don't worry, it's normal and we are here to help. Please email support@nvva.com.

All practices will start the week of December 2.

Travel teams are competitive teams. They are made up of girls with strong skill level and the desire to train and push themselves to their highest level of play.

This level has a demanding practice and tournament schedule. Athletes playing at this level have the desire to play in college and playing time is per the discretion of the coaches.

Travel Club fees include membership, practice court time, gear package, strength and conditioning training, coaches pay, and tournament entry, The fee does not include transportation/lodging fees for players.

Assistant Coach: James Burgess

Coach James has been coaching junior sports programs for over 15 years. He spent 12 years as a soccer coach and has been a volleyball assistant coach since 2015. He works closely with coach Esther Burgess to employ video technology to enable better insights to individual player and team performance.

Coach James Burgess works to help the athlete become more self-aware of where they are in their development as a player by focusing on the athletes’ technical skills for improved on-court results. During team play development, he focuses on helping the athlete increase their situational awareness so that each player on the court knows what to expect from their teammates during play.

Glenn arrived in Northern Virginia in May of 2008. He grew up in Colorado Springs, CO and graduated from the University of Northern Colorado (UNC) with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Psychology. An athlete's skills are developed through three phases; teaching, demonstration, and repetition. These phases are just as important in the development of one's life skills as well as volleyball skills. Glenn currently lives in Fairfax, VA and works for Fairfax County as the Director of Human Services Transportation.

12 Black

Regional teams are for athletes wanting to play on a club team that provides the very best coaching, training, and competition, but within the confines of a local/regional competition schedule (No competition further than 90 minutes away).

Regional Club fees include membership, practice court time, gear package, strength and conditioning training, coaches pay, and tournament entry. The fee does not include transportation/lodging fees for players. You can make your own plans on how to arrive to a tournament.

Teams will compete in 6 tournaments per season with two being multi-day tournaments. The teams will play two tournaments per month from January until the end of March.

At the Regional level, Black denotes our most competitive teams. After that is our red team then our white team. These teams are also competitive, but possibly have some players of less experience. Players are grouped together with other athletes based on similar athletic and volleyball skills as assessed at tryouts, as well as which position they may play on each team

Head Coach: Kory Borde

Coach Kory Borde has been a Head Coach for Club volleyball at Northern Virginia Volleyball Association since 2013. Coach Borde has Coached Club 14’s, 15’s and 16’s. He also Coaches Galaxy League ages 10-14 and runs the skills portion of the league to help ensure the development of all the younger athletes in the program.

Coach Borde has over 10 years of coaching experience. He has coached for NVVA as a head coach for Club, Galaxy, Volley Juniors and Loudoun Youth Volleyball leagues. He has also coached at Briar Woods High School as the Freshman Volleyball Head Coach 2013-2014 season where he lead the team to an 18-2 record and brought home the first volleyball trophy to the school. In addition to coaching volleyball, Coach Borde has coached soccer with Jefferson Youth Soccer Association in the American Youth Soccer Organization and Loudoun Soccer, Football with the AYFL and Baseball with DLL. He also has organized and supervised a number of volleyball tournaments, clinics and camps.
